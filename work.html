Annual Programs and Initiatives

Classroom Grants

Our teachers have the greatest impact on learning for our students and we want to make sure they have the resources they need. Each year, teachers apply for grants requesting things that would help them provide a better learning experience for their students. The funding provided for these grants is the most tangible way to elevate learning for every student in our building. Examples of past grants include:

  • Science Lab Organism Kits
  • Lift Chair for Special Education Classroom
  • Class Sets of AP Study Guides
  • Music Books for Orchestra and Band Programs

Schoolhouse Bricks

The art students of Norcross High School have painted a “school house” on canvas and teachers submit requests for items they need in their classrooms to enhance the learning experience for their students. The requests are written on “bricks” that are placed on the school house. During the annual Gala, members of the community purchase these “bricks” and the Foundation ensures that those funds are directed to that teacher or department for their specific need. This past spring, supporters purchased over $28,000 in bricks!

After School Matters (ASM) Program

This after school tutoring and mentoring program targets academically at-risk 9th and 10th grade students. The mission of the program is to raise the graduation rate and prepare students for career or college path. Students are tutored by certified Norcross High School teachers who help them develop study and organizational skills, and expose them to extra-curricular opportunities to increase school engagement.

ASM has yielded consistent results for the past four years. Enrolled students start each year with approximately 75% of the students failing one or more classes. By the end of the year, 95% of the students are passing all of their classes and a year closer to graduation!

Teachers Support and Recognition

As our faculty achieve levels of excellence, we want to make sure we recognize it and celebrate it. To encourage teacher innovation and teaching excellence, the Foundation annually confers Teacher of the Year Awards and Scholarships for teachers pursuing advanced degrees. Over 150 Norcross High School teachers hold Master's, Specialist, or Doctorate degrees.

Endowment Fund

The board has voted to set aside up to $5,000 per year (approved at the end of each year) to build an endowment with an eye toward longer-term investment and projects. While we, like most of you, prefer to see the majority of our giving spent while our children are enrolled at the school, we also wanted to establish a way to accommodate larger gifts from donors who may want to contribute toward the longer-term benefit of Norcross High.
